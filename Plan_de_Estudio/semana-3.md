| Sílaba  | Escritura                                 | Mnemotecnia                                    |
| ------- | ----------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------- |
| な (na) | た (ta) + un palito curvo a la derecha    | "NAda como un pez" — el palito extra es un pez |
| に (ni) | に = taquigrafía de 仁 (nin, persona)     | "NIño" — se parece al sombrero de un niño      |

| ね (ne) | ね = taquigrafía de 禰 (altar)            | "NEst" — el lazo parece un nido                |
| の (no) | Un círculo con un palito curvo            | "NO — el símbolo de prohibido"                 |

**Rutina:**

### 🎵 Música — Semicorcheas

Las semicorcheas duran **1/4 de pulso**. Cuatro semicorcheas = 1 negra.

| Figura             | Duración      | Cómo contarlo |
| ------------------ | ------------- | ------------- |
| ♩ (negra)          | 1 pulso       | "TA"          |
| ♬ (2 semicorcheas) | 1/2 pulso c/u | "TI-KA"       |
| ♫ (4 semicorcheas) | 1/4 pulso c/u | "TI-KA-TI-KA" |

**Práctica con metrónomo ♩=60:**

1. Contá "1-y-2-y-3-y-4-y" en voz alta (solo negras).
2. Después palmoteá en cada "y" (contratiempo).
3. Después palmoteá "TI-KA-TI-KA" en cada pulso.
4. Partitura con 4 compases: 2 compases de negras, 2 compases de semicorcheas.

### 🎵 Música — Compás 6/8

El 6/8 significa:

- **6** = seis tiempos por compás
- **8** = la corchea recibe 1 tiempo

**La cuenta:** ONE-two-three-FOUR-five-six
(Acento en el 1 y el 4 — se siente en DOS, no en seis.)

**Práctica con metrónomo ♩.=60 (dotted quarter):**

1. Contá "1-2-3-4-5-6" en voz alta con acento en 1 y 4.
2. Palmoteá solo en 1 y 4.
3. Palmoteá en 1, 2, 3... manteniendo el acento.

**Diferencia entre 4/4 y 6/8:**

| 4/4             | 6/8             |
| --------------- | --------------- |
| 1-2-3-4         | 1-2-3-4-5-6     |
| Pulso = negra   | Pulso = corchea |
| Acento en 1 y 3 | Acento en 1 y 4 |
| Se siente en 4  | Se siente en 2  |

## 📖 Grammar Reference — Week 3

### Have / Has Got (Possession)

|           | Positive             | Negative                | Question           |
| --------- | -------------------- | ----------------------- | ------------------ |
| I         | **have got** a phone | **haven't got** a phone | **Have** I got?    |
| You       | **have got** a car   | **haven't got** a car   | **Have** you got?  |
| He/She/It | **has got** a dog    | **hasn't got** a dog    | **Has** she got?   |
| We        | **have got** tickets | **haven't got** tickets | **Have** we got?   |
| They      | **have got** money   | **haven't got** money   | **Have** they got? |

**Short forms:** have got = 've got / has got = 's got / have not = haven't / has not = hasn't

**Remember:** Have/has got = TENER (poseer). NO se usa para acciones (no digas "I have got a shower" — decí "I take a shower").

### Present Continuous (Actions NOW)

|             | Positive         | Negative             | Question               |
| ----------- | ---------------- | -------------------- | ---------------------- |
| I           | **am studying**  | **am not studying**  | **Am** I studying?     |
| He/She/It   | **is studying**  | **is not studying**  | **Is** she studying?   |
| You/We/They | **are studying** | **are not studying** | **Are** they studying? |

**Formula:** am/is/are + verb + -ing

**Spelling rules for -ing:**
| Verb ending | Rule | Example |
|-------------|------|---------|
| Most verbs | Add -ing | play → playing, study → studying |
| -e (silent e) | Remove -e, add -ing | write → writing, make → making |
| Short vowel + consonant | Double consonant, add -ing | swim → swimming, run → running |
| -ie | Change -ie to -y, add -ing | lie → lying, die → dying |

**When to use Present Continuous:**

- Right now: "I **am studying** at the moment."
- Temporary: "She **is living** with her aunt this month."
- Trend: "More people **are learning** Japanese."
- With 'look!', 'listen!': "Look! It **is snowing**."

**Not used with** (stative verbs): believe, know, like, love, hate, want, need, understand, remember, belong.

✅ "I **believe** you." (NOT "I am believing")
✅ "She **likes** ice cream." (NOT "She is liking")

### 🎵 Music Reference — Week 3

| Concept                   | What it means                    | How to count                   |
| ------------------------- | -------------------------------- | ------------------------------ |
| **Sixteenth note (𝅘𝅥𝅯𝅰)** | 1/4 beat                         | "TI-KA-TI-KA" (four per pulse) |
| **6/8 time**              | 6 beats per bar, eighth = 1 beat | 1-2-3-4-5-6 (accent 1 and 4)   |
| **Bass clef (𝄢)**         | Low notes (left hand on piano)   | Lines: GBDFA. Spaces: ACEG.    |
| **Treble clef (𝄞)**       | High notes (right hand)          | Lines: EGBDF. Spaces: FACE.    |
| **Dotted quarter (𝅘𝅥.)**   | = three eighth notes in 6/8      | One beat in 6/8 (weighted)     |

**Bass clef mnemonic:**

- Lines (bottom to top): **G**ood **B**oys **D**eserve **F**udge **A**lways
- Spaces (bottom to top): **A**ll **C**ows **E**at **G**rass

**4/4 vs 6/8 comparison:**

```
4/4: 1-2-3-4 | ♩ ♩ ♩ ♩ | feels square, march-like
6/8: 1-2-3-4-5-6 | ♪ ♪ ♪ ♪ ♪ ♪ | feels round, rolling, waltz-like
```

**Key difference:** in 4/4 you feel 4 pulses. In 6/8 you feel 2 (the 1 and the 4).
