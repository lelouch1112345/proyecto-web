#### 💡 Real Teaching Content

**The Science**: Spaced repetition exploits the **spacing effect** (Ebbinghaus, 1885; Cepeda et al., 2006) — information reviewed at expanding intervals is retained dramatically longer than massed practice. The forgetting curve drops steeply within 24 hours, then plateaus. Each successful retrieval resets the curve at a shallower slope.

**Concrete Application to Your Plan:**

Your Anki setup currently has generic decks. Here's how to design SPECIFIC intervals for each subject:

| Subject            | Initial Interval                        | Graduating Interval | Why                                                                      |
| ------------------ | --------------------------------------- | ------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| English vocabulary | 4 min → 1h → 1d → 3d → 7d → 14d → 30d   | 21d                 | English cognates are easier — faster expansion                           |
| Japanese hiragana  | 1 min → 10min → 1d → 2d → 4d → 8d → 15d | 15d                 | Hiragana needs faster initial reviews because there's NO cognate support |
| Music note ID      | 2 min → 30min → 1d → 2d → 5d → 10d      | 20d                 | Physical spacing (treble vs bass clef) needs mid-range intervals         |
| Rhythm patterns    | Same-session review only                | 7d                  | Muscle memory consolidates via sleep, not same-day repetition            |

**Critical Rule for a 16-year-old**: NEVER let your review backlog exceed 50 cards. If it does, stop adding new cards and only review for 48h. Adolescence has peak neuroplasticity BUT also peak distractibility.

**Implementation**: Add these deck presets in Anki:

1. Deck "Inglés" → graduating interval 21d, easy bonus 130%, max interval 90d
2. Deck "Japonés" → graduating interval 15d, easy bonus 120%, max interval 60d


#### 💡 Real Teaching Content

**The 20 Rules of Formulating Knowledge (Wozniak, 1999) distilled for a 16-year-old:**

1. **ONE fact per card.** NOT "town = pueblo, city = ciudad" on one card. Two cards: "town → pueblo" AND "city → ciudad."

2. **Minimize the answer space.** If the answer can be 1 word, don't make it a sentence. `Anverso: town → Reverso: pueblo` is better than `Reverso: "pueblo/ciudad pequeña — My town is small"`. Save full sentences for PRODUCTION cards.

3. **No cloze deletions for vocabulary.** Cloze ("My \_\_\_ is small") tests recall of the WORD within context. This is SLOWER for initial vocabulary acquisition. Use Basic (front/back) for first encounter, then cloze for consolidation.

4. **Use mnemonics in the EXTRA field**, not the answer. Anki has a field for "Extra." Put `🔗 Mnemonic: Town tiene "ow" como "owl" — un búho vive en un pueblo` there. DO NOT put it in the answer field. Read the mnemonic only if you forget.

5. **Audio on EVERY English card.** Install AwesomeTTS add-on. Every card auto-plays pronunciation. For a 16-year-old, hearing the word is non-negotiable — you're building the auditory AND visual pathways simultaneously.

**Anki Add-ons for your stack:**

- AwesomeTTS (text-to-speech on review)
- Heatmap (visual streak tracker — motivating)
- Review Heatmap (shows your consistency)

**Your daily Anki workflow:**

```
1. Morning (after waking): 5 min — REVIEW only, NO new cards
2. After study session: add 5-10 new cards
3. Before bed: 5 min — REVIEW only
```

**Morning reviews should ALWAYS come before new cards.** Retrieval is strongest after sleep consolidation.

- After each study session, close everything and write (or speak) for 2 min:

**Specific to your music practice:**

```
MÓDULO DE APRENDIZAJE EXPERTO: Micro-Commitments Strategy
Category: 3. Self-Mastery & Emotional > Self-Discipline
```

#### 💡 Real Teaching Content

**The Science**: Micro-commitments (Fogg, 2019 — Tiny Habits) reduce the activation energy needed to start. The smaller the commitment, the less willpower required. For a 16-year-old brain, where the prefrontal cortex is still developing, this is CRITICAL.

**The 2-Minute Rule applied to your plan:**

Your plan already has micro-tasks (5 min Anki, 10 min Kodály). But on days when motivation is ZERO, even 5 min feels like 5 hours.

**Micro-commitment ladder for low-motivation days:**

| Day type       | Minimum commitment     | Success likely  |
| -------------- | ---------------------- | --------------- |
| 💪 High energy | Full plan (40 min)     | 95%             |
| 😐 Medium      | 20 min (half plan)     | 80%             |
| 😩 Low energy  | 5 min (just Anki)      | 60%             |
| 🤒 Very low    | 2 min (just one thing) | 90% — yes, 90%! |

**The paradox**: On low days, committing to 2 min is MORE likely to succeed than trying full plan and failing. AND once you START for 2 min, you often continue for 20+ min (the Zeigarnik effect — your brain wants to finish what it started).

**Three concrete micro-commitments for your plan:**

1. **English on zero days**: "I will read ONE text from British Council A2 and add exactly ONE word to Anki." That's it. The bar is on the floor. You WILL do this. And once you're on the site, you'll probably do more.

2. **Japanese on zero days**: "I will open my hiragana Anki deck and do EXACTLY 5 cards." Not 20. Five.

3. **Music on zero days**: "I will open MusicTheory.net and name 5 notes." Five. In 30 seconds.

**The commitment device**: Tell someone (or write publicly) what your floor is. "I commit to 2 min of English every day." Missing 2 min feels worse than doing 2 min. And 2 min is always doable.

### Skill 3.3 — Flow State Triggers Calibration

**Engine Output (teach):**

```
MÓDULO DE APRENDIZAJE EXPERTO: Flow State Triggers Calibration
Category: 3. Self-Mastery & Emotional > Focus & Attention
```

#### 💡 Real Teaching Content

**The Science**: Flow (Csikszentmihalyi, 1990) requires a balance between challenge and skill. If challenge > skill → anxiety. If skill > challenge → boredom. Flow = just-right challenge.

**Flow triggers for YOUR study sessions:**

| Activity             | Current Skill    | Challenge  | Flow Gap                                   | Adjustment                  |
| -------------------- | ---------------- | ---------- | ------------------------------------------ | --------------------------- |
| English reading A2   | A2 (you're here) | A2 text    | ✅ FLOW                                    | Keep current level          |
| English listening A1 | A1               | A1 audio   | ✅ FLOW                                    | Keep current level          |
| Note ID treble clef  | Variable         | Same notes | ⚠️ BOREDOM after 3 days                    | Expand range OR reduce time |
| Kodály rhythm        | Beginner         | TA, TI-TI  | ✅ FLOW first 3 days → ⚠️ BOREDOM by day 5 | Add 3/4 time on day 4       |

**The 4-day flow calibration cycle:**

```
Day 1: New skill → Challenge > Skill (slight anxiety) → PUSH THROUGH
Day 2: Skill catching up → Challenge ≈ Skill → FLOW 🎯
Day 3: Mastery → Skill > Challenge → FLOW continues
Day 4: Boredom → Skill >> Challenge → CHANGE difficulty
```

**Your plan already follows this pattern intuitively!** Kodály starts with TA for 1 day, adds TI-TI on day 2, 3/4 time in week 2. This is natural flow calibration.

**Explicit upgrade: Pre-session calibration check**

Before each session, ask yourself:

> "On a scale of 1-10, how bored or anxious am I about this task?"

| Score               | Meaning            | Action                                      |
| ------------------- | ------------------ | ------------------------------------------- |
| 1-3 (very bored)    | Skill >> challenge | Make it harder (faster tempo, no subtitles) |
| 4-6 (comfortable)   | Skill ≈ challenge  | ✅ Flow — continue                          |
| 7-8 (a bit anxious) | Challenge > skill  | Make it easier (use subtitles, slow down)   |
| 9-10 (overwhelmed)  | Challenge >> skill | Drop a level (A1→pre-A1, or rest)           |

This takes 10 seconds and prevents the two main study killers: boredom (1-3) and overwhelm (9-10).
